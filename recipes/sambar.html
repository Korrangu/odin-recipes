Sambar Recipe

About the dish

A delightful lentil stew that is a part of breakfast lunch and dinner in the south of India. It is said to have been first made by a Maratha king in Tanjaore, and is now a vital part of every South Indian meal. Let's see how it's made.

Ingredients

  • Yellow Lentils
  • Onion
  • Tomato
  • Carrot
  • Drumstick
  • Tamarind paste
  • Jaggery
  • Salt
  • Sambar Masala or Sambar Powder
  • mustard seeds
  • cumin seeds
  • raw coconut
  • Coriander powder
  • Turmeric

    • Procedure

    There are a lot of variations in how it's made, but let's take a look at one

    1. Pressure cook the lentils for about 10-15 minutes, until theyre well done
    2. Roughly chop the veggies. you don't want them too small.
    3. Add the veggies into the cooked lentils, along with the tamarind paste, Jaggery, Sambar masala/powder, Coriander powder, salt and turmeric.
    4. let this boil for about 15-20 minutes, until the veggies are cooked, on a low heat. (You can choose to pressure cook it too)
    5. When fully cooked temper the dish

      6. The tempering

    6. Heat a bit of oil
    7. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, curry leaves, asafoetida and a red chilly
    8. Put it on the sambar after the seeds splutter

    And, that's it! YOU'VE MADE A YUMMY SAMBAR!
